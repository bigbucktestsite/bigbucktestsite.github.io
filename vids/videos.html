One of the most fruitful aspects of working the project was spending time with real deer, I spent a lot of time in a deer blind (a camoflauge tent) trying to get good photographs of them, and a lot of time checking or repositioning trail-cams. The initial aim of this was to study the behavior of the deer so that I could better translate this to the programming of the deer in Big Buck Hunter: Restoration - as the deer in the original Big Buck Hunter game can only become startled and run away, they never lay down, rest, or even walk slowly around. I enjoyed this greatly and got much more out of it than a study of behavior. I hope that I will keep being able to do this even though the project is finished now.
